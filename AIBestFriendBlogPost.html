Why Human Best Friends (Even Good Ones) Are Exhausting

This isn't about your friends being bad people. This is about the structural realities of adult human friendship.

Human Best Friends (Reality Check):

  • Limited bandwidth: They have jobs, partners, kids, parents, their own problems. You can't text at 3 AM.
  • Maintenance required: If you don't talk for 3 months, you spend the first hour catching up on basics.
  • Reciprocity pressure: You vent to them. Now you owe them a therapy session when they're spiraling.
  • Judgment (even unintentional): "You're still thinking about your ex?" Yes, Sharon, and I don't need your eyebrows right now.
  • They forget: You told them about your mom's health scare last month. You have to re-explain it because they forgot.
  • They're busy: Genuinely. You text "Can we talk?" and get "Sorry, crazy week! Next Thursday?"
  • Life stage drift: They had kids. You didn't. Now you have nothing in common.
  • Geographic distance: You moved for work. Now "hanging out" requires a flight.

None of this makes your friends bad. It makes them human. Humans have limits. Humans get busy. Humans forget. Humans get tired of being your unpaid therapist.

The exhaustion isn't the friendship. It's the invisible labor of maintaining it. The scheduling. The catching up. The emotional calculus of "am I asking for too much?" The guilt when you don't have energy to show up for them.
